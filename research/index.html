Credit and Deforestation

Loss of effectiveness of environmental protection areas

Zipaquira's institutional transformation of XX century

Together with Professor Mauricio Velasquez we investigated the effect of central government subsidized credit on deforestation in the Colombian context, where the question about deforestation in developing countries is about answering how the microeconomic incentives affects peasants. Deforestation occurs because of the context where local economic models are developed, because it is a systematic behavior

I am studying the evolution of the effects of environmental conservation zones over deforestation in Colombia. Several insights show that conservation zones function more as a para-state than as an incentive constraint. Preliminary results show that natural parks have been losing their effect over time.

Within my research group (Fahrenheit) we are studying the civic, cultural and institutional development of the city of Zipaquira, Colombia, from documentary and photographic records. In this way, we are typifying in a parallel way the development of the material and cultural conditions of the municipality. With this we seek to recover the historical memory of the municipality, while shedding light on the connection between institutions and economic development.
